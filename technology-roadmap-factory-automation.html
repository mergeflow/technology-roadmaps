Technology Roadmap on Factory Automation

1. Introduction

Overview

Factory automation refers to the use of technology and control systems to automate manufacturing processes and improve efficiency, productivity, and safety in a factory setting. It involves the integration of various hardware and software components to create a seamless and efficient production line.

Working Principle

The working principle of factory automation involves the use of various technologies such as sensors, actuators, programmable logic controllers (PLCs), human-machine interfaces (HMIs), and robotics to automate the manufacturing process. These components work together to monitor and control the production line, ensuring that each step is executed accurately and efficiently.

Sensors are used to detect changes in the environment, such as the presence of a product or the level of a material in a container. Actuators are used to control the movement of machines and equipment, such as opening and closing valves or moving conveyor belts. PLCs are used to control the overall operation of the production line, including the sequencing of machines and the monitoring of sensors and actuators. HMIs provide a graphical interface for operators to monitor and control the production line, while robotics are used to automate tasks such as assembly, packaging, and material handling.

By automating the manufacturing process, factory automation can improve efficiency, reduce costs, and increase safety in the factory setting. It can also enable manufacturers to produce higher quality products at a faster rate, leading to increased competitiveness in the market.
