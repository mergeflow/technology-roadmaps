Technology Roadmap on Microplate Readers

1. Introduction

Overview

Microplate readers are laboratory instruments used to detect and measure biological, chemical or physical events in microtiter plates. They are widely used in life science research, drug discovery, clinical diagnostics, and quality control testing in various industries.

Working Principle

Microplate readers work by measuring the absorbance, fluorescence, or luminescence of samples in microtiter plates. The microtiter plates contain multiple wells, each of which can hold a small volume of sample. The instrument uses a light source to illuminate the samples in the wells, and then detects the amount of light that is absorbed, emitted, or scattered by the samples. The amount of light detected is proportional to the amount of the target molecule or analyte present in the sample. The data obtained from the microplate reader can be used to quantify the concentration of the target molecule or analyte, or to monitor changes in biological or chemical processes over time.

Future trends in anti-ferroptotic therapeutics

Receptor-Independent Anti-Ferroptotic Activity of TrkB Modulators
19.12.2022
  • TrkB modulators such as 7,8-DHF, phenelzine sulphate, and GNF-5837 could be potential anti-ferroptotic therapeutics for various diseases.
  • These TrkB modulators show anti-ferroptotic activity via a receptor-independent radical-trapping antioxidant mechanism.
  • Caution should be applied when interpreting the results of these compounds in prior and future studies since the potent radical-trapping antioxidant effect may confound the result.
