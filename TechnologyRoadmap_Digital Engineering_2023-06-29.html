Technology Roadmap on Digital Engineering

1. Introduction

Overview

Digital engineering is a field that involves the use of digital tools and techniques to design, simulate, and analyze complex systems. It encompasses a wide range of disciplines, including computer-aided design (CAD), computer-aided engineering (CAE), and computer-aided manufacturing (CAM).

Working Principle

The working principle of digital engineering involves the use of computer software and hardware to create, test, and optimize designs. This process typically begins with the creation of a digital model or prototype using CAD software. The model can then be analyzed and optimized using CAE software to ensure that it meets the desired performance specifications. Once the design is finalized, CAM software can be used to generate instructions for manufacturing the product. Digital engineering allows for faster and more accurate design iterations, as well as the ability to simulate and test designs under a wide range of conditions before physical prototypes are built.
