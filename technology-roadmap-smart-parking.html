Technology Roadmap on Smart Parking

1. Introduction

Overview

Smart parking is a technology that uses sensors, cameras, and other devices to monitor parking spaces and provide real-time information to drivers about available parking spots. It aims to reduce traffic congestion, save time and fuel, and improve the overall parking experience for drivers.

Working Principle

The working principle of smart parking involves the use of various sensors and cameras that are installed in parking lots or on the streets. These sensors detect the presence or absence of vehicles in parking spaces and transmit this information to a central system. The system then processes this data and provides real-time information to drivers about available parking spots through mobile apps, electronic signs, or other means. This information can also be used to optimize parking operations, such as identifying areas with high demand and adjusting pricing accordingly. Smart parking can also be integrated with other technologies, such as automated payment systems and electric vehicle charging stations, to provide a seamless parking experience for drivers.
