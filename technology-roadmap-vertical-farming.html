Technology Roadmap on Vertical Farming

1. Introduction

Overview

Vertical farming is a method of growing crops in vertically stacked layers, using artificial lighting and controlled environment agriculture technology. This technique allows for year-round crop production, higher crop yields, and efficient use of space and resources.

Working Principle

Vertical farming involves growing crops in vertically stacked layers, using artificial lighting and controlled environment agriculture technology. The plants are grown in a soilless medium, such as hydroponics or aeroponics, and are fed with a nutrient-rich solution. The environment is carefully controlled to optimize plant growth, with factors such as temperature, humidity, and light intensity all carefully monitored and adjusted as needed. The use of LED lighting allows for precise control over the light spectrum, which can be tailored to the specific needs of the plants being grown. Vertical farming can be done indoors or outdoors, and can be used to grow a wide variety of crops, including leafy greens, herbs, and even fruiting crops like tomatoes and strawberries.
